    Gary Gilbert is an American film producer and business executive who has a net worth of $400 million. He is the president and founder of the motion picture production and financing company Gilbert Films out of Los Angeles, California. He is also the co-owner of the NBA's Cleveland Cavaliers with his brother Dan Gilbert.

    Gary Gilbert was born in 1965. Gary Gilbert graduated from the University of Michigan's Ross School of Business.

    He has produced or executive produced the movies "Garden State" in 2004, "Henry Poole Is Here" in 2008, "Pathology" in 2008, "The Kids Are All Right" in 2010, "Meet Monica Velour" in 2010, "From Prada to Nada" in 2011, "Margaret" in 2011, "Save the Date" in 2011, "Are You Here" in 2013, "Grounded" in 2013, "The Keeping Room" in 2014, "Miss Stevens" in 2016, "The Cleanse" in 2016, "Little Boxes" in 2016, and "La La Land" in 2016. In 2011 he was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Motion Picture of the Year for "The Kids Are All Right."
