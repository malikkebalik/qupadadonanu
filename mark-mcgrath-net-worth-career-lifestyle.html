Mark McGrath | Career

He began his musical career in 1992 when he formed the band “Sugar Ray.”

Sugar Ray was noted in the early years for producing Nu-Metal and was once known as the Shrinky Dinx.

After signing a record deal with Atlantic in 1994, the band eventually started to embrace a more popular sound, discarding its Nu-Metal roots.

Their debut album “Lemonade and Brownies” didn’t make much of an impression in mainstream society, but it got people talking in alternative and underground communities.

Sugar Ray had far more popularity with their 1997 album “Floored,” which included “Fly,” a mainstream hit.

Despite the fact that many critics thought Sugar Ray was a one-hit-wonder, the band released “14:59” in 1999.

The project was a great success with hit singles like “Every Morning” and “Someday.”

Furthermore, he released a solo EP named “Summertime’s Coming” in 2015.
