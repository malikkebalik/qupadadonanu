Where does the citric acid cycle occur in bacteria?

Publish date: 2024-07-05

In prokaryotic cells, the citric acid cycle occurs in the cytoplasm; in eukaryotic cells the citric acid cycle takes place in the matrix of the mitochondria.Click to see full answer. Besides, where does glycolysis and the citric acid cycle occur in bacterial cells?Both types of metabolism share the initial pathway of glycolysis, but aerobic metabolism continues with the Krebs cycle and oxidative phosphorylation.
