Robert Mugabe was a former Zimbabwean politician and revolutionary who gained fame for his efforts to resist British rule during the era when Zimbabwe was known as Rhodesia.

Mugabe was deeply influenced by Marxist and nationalist ideologies from an early age and rose to become the publicity secretary of the National Democratic Party (NDP).

He went on to found the socialist-nationalist movement, ZANU, which was dedicated to liberating Zimbabwe from British control.

After Zimbabwe achieved independence, Mugabe became the country’s first prime minister and, later, its president, a position he held until November 21, 2017.

During his time as president, Mugabe worked to unite the Zimbabwe African People’s Union (ZAPU) with the Zimbabwe African National Union (ZANU).

He staunchly opposed any attempts to re-colonize Zimbabwe.

However, Mugabe’s policies began to face opposition, and his electoral campaigns were criticized for being marred by fraud and violence.

This eventually led to growing discontent with his rule, and a coup in November 2017 forced Mugabe to step down from the presidency.
